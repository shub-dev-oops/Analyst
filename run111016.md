

**RUN-111016 – Swagit Agenda Frame Incident (Short Summary)**

* **What happened:**
  Swagit-admin-v3 release (accessibility + Media Manager prep) changed shared layout/CSS and iframe sizing. In PROD this shrunk the agenda frame (~275px height), removed scrollbars, and broke transcript layout across *all* Swagit players, making agendas effectively unusable.

* **Key metrics:**
  * **Incident Start (formal):** 14 Nov, 12:21 PM CT
  * **Incident Awareness:** 12:22 PM CT
  * **Incident Fixed**: 2:00 PM CT
  * **Severity:** SEV-3 (feature unusable, platform up)
  * **Formal incident window:** 12:21 PM–2:00 PM CT
  * **MTTA:** ~1 min (from incident creation)
  * **MTTR:** 99 min
  * Practically, customers saw degraded UI from ~7:00 AM CT until ~2:00 PM CT (≈ 7 hours).

---

### Why it occurred (despite QE “green”)

1. **Dev/QE gap:**

   * QE validated in INT, but regression scope didn’t fully cover agenda/transcript layout and scrollbars across multiple real-world player variants.
   * INT/UAT didn’t mirror real customer layouts/themes, so the global CSS/layout change looked fine in lower envs but broke in PROD.

2. **SRE/operational gap:**

   * No synthetic or automated checks for “agenda panel usable and scrollable” – we monitor up/down, not UI usability, I assume in elastic.

---

### How it could have been prevented

* **Dev/QE side:**

  * Stronger regression checklist for any global CSS/accessibility change (agenda size, transcript panel, scrollbars, multiple player versions).
  * Better INT/UAT parity with “golden” customer tenants or layouts, and/or feature-flagged / phased rollout instead of all-at-once.

* **SRE side:**

  * Add UI-focused synthetic checks for Swagit players (agenda height, scrollbars, transcript behavior) as a gate and post-release health check.
  * Define feature-level SLI/SLO for “agenda usability” and introduce mandatory post-release smoke tests for Swagit-admin releases for the complete UI testing.
